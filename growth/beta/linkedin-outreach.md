# LinkedIn Outreach Strategy

LinkedIn is a high-quality channel for finding designers, developers, and career switchers with urgency.

---

## Who to target on LinkedIn

Use LinkedIn search with these filters:

| Target | Search terms | Filter |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| New grads | "open to work" + recent grad | Graduated in last 12 months |
| Career switchers | "open to work" + coding bootcamp / design bootcamp | 2nd connections |
| Job-seeking designers | "UX designer" + "open to work" | 1–3 years experience |
| Job-seeking developers | "software engineer" + "open to work" | 0–4 years experience |

**Do not:** use LinkedIn Sales Navigator scraping, bulk connection requests, or any automation tool.

---

## Connection request approach

1. Write a personalized connection note (≤300 chars) that references something specific about them
2. Wait for them to accept — never send a DM to a pending connection
3. Within 24 hours of connecting, send the relevant DM from `direct-dm-scripts.md`
4. If no response after 5 days: send one more short follow-up, then stop

**Max connection requests per day:** 15 (to avoid LinkedIn flagging your account)

---

## Profile optimization (do this before outreach)

Your LinkedIn profile should clearly state you're the founder of Showcase and explain what it does in the headline or about section. People check your profile before responding.

Suggested headline:
> Building Showcase — AI portfolio generator for designers, developers, and career switchers

---

## What to track

In `beta-user-tracker.csv`, note:
- Source: `linkedin`
- How you found them (search, mutual connection, comment)
- Connection date and DM sent date
- Response and outcome

---

## Success criteria

Aim for:
- 40%+ connection acceptance rate (if lower, your note is too generic)
- 30%+ reply rate from accepted connections (if lower, the DM isn't resonating)
- 20%+ conversion from reply to beta signup
