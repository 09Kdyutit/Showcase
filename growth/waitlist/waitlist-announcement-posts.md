---

**Post 2 — What ProofScore actually measures**
Most portfolio advice is vague. "Be specific." "Show your impact." "Quantify everything."

ProofScore goes further. It audits your portfolio across 11 dimensions:

1. First Impression Clarity — does anyone know who you are in 5 seconds?
2. Target Role Alignment — is everything pointing at the job you actually want?
3. Proof Strength — what percentage of your claims have real evidence?
4. Project Depth — do your case studies show problem + process + outcome?
5. Resume Quality — are your bullets clear, specific, and results-focused?
6. Case Study Quality — would a recruiter find this compelling?
7. Credibility Signals — awards, projects, notable companies, publications
8. Visual Polish — does the presentation match your positioning?
9. Contact Readiness — can a recruiter reach you and research you easily?
10. Keyword Relevance — does your language match the jobs you are applying for?
11. Hiring Risk Gaps — vague dates, responsibility-only bullets, unexplained gaps

Most portfolios score below 60. The ones that get responses tend to score above 80.
